📚 Chapter-wise Topics for Today

⚡ Physics – Electric Charges & Fields (4-5 marks)
MUST Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
Point charges, calculation of force, superposition principle. Ask graphical representation.
MUST Electric Field Definition & Calculation
E = F/q, field due to point charge, field direction. Frequently asked numerical.
MUST Superposition Principle
Net field from multiple charges, vector addition. Complex circuit problems.
IMP Electric Potential & Work Done
V = W/q, potential difference. Work in moving charge from A to B.
IMP Potential Energy & Equipotential
U = kq₁q₂/r, equipotential surface concept, relationship with field.
MED Electric Flux & Gauss Law
Concept understanding required, rarely asked numerically.
🔬 Chemistry – Chemical Kinetics (4-5 marks)
MUST Rate of Reaction Definition & Calculation
-d[A]/dt formulation, concentration vs time data interpretation. Numerical MCQ most asked.
MUST Order of Reaction (0, 1st, 2nd Order)
Determining order from data, rate law formulation. Complete problem-type coverage needed.
MUST Rate Constant k & Its Calculation
k = Rate/[A]ⁿ, integrated rate equations, half-life formula: t₁/₂ = 0.693/k (1st order).
IMP Arrhenius Equation: k = Ae⁻ᴱᵃ/ᴿᵀ
Temperature effect, activation energy, frequency factor. Graph-based questions.
IMP Half-life & Reaction Characteristics
t₁/₂ concept, fractional lifetimes, practical applications.
MED Mechanism of Reaction & Catalyst
Elementary steps, catalyst effect on activation energy.
📐 Maths – Matrices & Determinants (6-7 marks)
MUST Determinant Calculation (2×2 & 3×3)
|A| = ad-bc formula, Sarrus rule for 3×3. Every exam has 2-3 direct questions.
MUST Matrix Operations (Addition, Multiplication)
Matrix algebra properties, commutativity, distributive laws. Problem-based MCQ.
MUST Inverse Matrix: A⁻¹ = adj(A)/det(A)
Adjugate matrix calculation, existence condition (det≠0). Application in solving equations.
IMP Cramer's Rule for Linear Equations
Solving system of 2-3 equations using determinants. Direct application questions.
IMP Properties of Determinants
|AB| = |A||B|, |A⁻¹| = 1/|A|, effect of row/column operations.
MED Transpose & Symmetric Matrices
(AB)ᵀ = BᵀAᵀ, A = Aᵀ condition. Theoretical understanding.

📐 Formula Sheet – Day 2 Chapters

⚡ Electric Charges & Fields – Physics
COULOMB'S LAW
F = k × q₁ × q₂ / r²
(k = 9×10⁹ N⋅m²/C²)

ELECTRIC FIELD
E = F / q
E = k × Q / r²

ELECTRIC POTENTIAL
V = W / q
V = k × q / r

POTENTIAL DIFFERENCE
V_AB = V_A - V_B
W = q × V_AB

POTENTIAL ENERGY
U = k × q₁ × q₂ / r

WORK DONE
W = q(V_B - V_A)
W = ΔU
🔬 Chemical Kinetics – Chemistry
RATE OF REACTION
Rate = -d[A]/dt (Concentration change)

RATE LAW
Rate = k[A]ⁿ[B]ᵐ
Order = n + m

RATE CONSTANT
k = Rate / [A]ⁿ

FIRST ORDER
ln[A] = ln[A]₀ - kt
t₁/₂ = 0.693 / k

ARRHENIUS EQUATION
k = A × e⁻ᴱᵃ/ᴿᵀ
ln(k) = ln(A) - Ea/RT

HALF-LIFE
For 1st order: t₁/₂ = 0.693/k (constant)
For 2nd order: t₁/₂ = 1/k[A]₀ (varies)
📐 Matrices & Determinants – Maths
DETERMINANT 2×2
|A| = ad - bc (for matrix [[a,b],[c,d]])

MATRIX MULTIPLICATION PROPERTY
|AB| = |A| × |B|

INVERSE MATRIX
A⁻¹ = adj(A) / |A|
(exists only if |A| ≠ 0)

TRANSPOSE PROPERTY
(AB)ᵀ = BᵀAᵀ

CRAMER'S RULE (2×2 System)
x = |A_x|/|A|, y = |A_y|/|A|

DETERMINANT PROPERTIES
|A⁻¹| = 1/|A|
|kA| = kⁿ|A| (n = order)

ℹ️ Quick Cheat Codes – Chapter Endings (Score 45+)

Physics Tricks: Coulomb force always attractive for unlike charges. Potential goes higher near positive charges. Electric field lines NEVER cross or form closed loops.

Chemistry Tricks: Half-life is constant ONLY for 1st order. Rate constant k increases with temperature ALWAYS. Higher order = faster change in rate with concentration.

Maths Tricks: Determinant = 0 means matrix is singular (no inverse). |AB| = |A|×|B| always. For Cramer's rule to work, |A| must NOT be 0.
